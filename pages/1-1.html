Change of Variable

08 Aug 2025

1. Change of Variable for Density

\begin{equation} p(\mathbf{x}) = \pi (f^{-1} (\mathbf{x})) \cdot \left|\frac{\partial f^{-1}}{\partial \mathbf{x}} \right| \end{equation} for one-to-one $f$.

2. Change of Variable for Distribution

\begin{equation} \int_{f (\mathcal{Z})} g(x) d \mathbf{x} = \int_{\mathcal{Z}} g( f(\mathbf{z})) \left|\frac{\partial \mathbf{x}}{\partial \mathbf{z}} \right| d \mathbf{z} \end{equation}
Example: Entropy Calculation
\begin{equation} H[\mathbf{y}] = - \int p_{\mathbf{y}} (y) \log p_{\mathbf{y}} (y) dy \end{equation} Now, under the linear transformation $y=Ax$ \begin{equation} H[\mathbf{y}] = - \int \left(\frac{p_{\mathbf{x} (x)}}{\text{det} (A)}\right) \log \left( \frac{p_{\mathbf{x}}(x)}{\text{det} (A)}\right) \left|\frac{\partial y}{\partial x} \right| dx \end{equation} $\implies$ $H[\mathbf{y}] = H[\mathbf{x}] + \log \text{det}(A)$
Therefore in the case that $X$ is continuous, then the differential entropy of Y is $not$ the same as the differential entropy of $X$.
